Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family to attend university of Minnesota.
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save mmoney. Works in Civilian Conservation Corps.
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in Forestry Department.
  • 1938 - Laid off due to budegt cuts. He returns to school study under Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistant plants.
  • 1941 - Military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants and other applied sciences.
  • 1942 - Receives a Ph.D in Genetics and Plant pathology.
  • 1944 - Flies to Mexico to head a new plant pathology program.
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to double wheat yields.
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high yielding strains of wheat to Indian subcontinent.
  • 1970 - Receives Nobel Peace prize.
  • 1983 - Helps 7 African countries to double their maize yields.
  • 1984 - Becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University.
  • 2009 - Dies at age 95.
